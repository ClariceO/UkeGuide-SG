1. Tune your ukulele

All ukuleles must be in a particular pitch in order to play music in modern scales. Therefore we must tune our ukuleles.
Below is a ukulele tuner, click the the play button to start tuning by playing each string and adjust the tension accordingly till it sounds similar!

Each note will be played 3 times. Click a button and start tuning!

Pause by clicking the button again to play the next note




The most popular and common tuning is known as the gCEA. The gCEA tuning makes it easy to play in C major key, which is a key used by many songs.

There is also a D tuning, which is actually gCEA but shifted 2 frets up - aDF#B. This tuning is more popular in the 1920s to 30s.

Chicago tuning also known as DGBE tuning is more popular for larger ukuleles, more common on the baritone ukes. These 4 notes matches with the 1st 4 strings on the guitar, allowing simple guitar - ukulele song translating.

Lastly, fA#DG — A#/Bb tuning is the less common tuning but it is useful for playing lower pitches to fit lower songs or to make singing along easier.
