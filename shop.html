4 Main Types of Ukulele

This ukulele has a standard length of 21 inches which is the smallest type of ukulele. It has a standard tuning of G-C-E-A. Keeping in mind that "the smaller the body the higher the pitch" the soprano ukuleles are higher in pitch. This type of ukulele is for you if you are only using it for strumming chords and if you prefer your uke to be light-weight.

This ukulele is around 23 inches and has a standard tuning of G-C-E-A as well. Compared to the Soprano, it has a bigger body and a longer, wider neck. This ukulele is for you if you like something light-weight and at the same time sound very traditional. The concert is also a very safe one to choose if you are ordering your uke online.

The tenor ukulele is arouind 30 inches. Like the soprano and the concert ukuleles it also has a standard tuning of G-C-E-A. It is bigger, wider and longer compared to the concert ukulele. This means that the sound of it is louder and lower. Tenor allows players to play chords and single notes easily due to the longer neck. Therefore, this ukulele is for you if you would like to learn how to play single notes as well as chords.

Like the tenor ukulele, the baritone is also around 30 inches but broader. It plays a lower pitch and is tuned to D-G-B-E instead. Hence, if you want to look for pieces to play, make sure that the notes are for the baritone's tuning. This ukulele is even larger, it has a bigger body and a wider, longer neck compared to the tenor ukulele, it also sounds deeper. This ukulele is for you if you like more base and want this specific tune. Remember, it has a different tuning!
